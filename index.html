    Mspice is a Python-based SPICE engine built for students and researchers — lightweight, portable, and fully open-source.

    About Us

    We are a team of four senior students from the Department of Electrical Engineering at National Central University (NCU), Taiwan. This project, "Mspice," serves as our graduation capstone project, developed under the guidance of Prof. Chiu-Chih Chou.

    Why Mspice? While professional tools like Hspice are powerful, they can be intimidating for newcomers. Leveraging Python's intuitive syntax , we built this SPICE engine from scratch to gain a deeper understanding of circuit analysis algorithms. Our goal is to create a lightweight, beginner-friendly, and open-source tool that makes circuit simulation accessible to everyone—without the steep learning curve.

    Get-started

    Mspice uses SPICE netlists—text files describing circuits. Think of it as a recipe: you list components, connections, and cooking instructions (simulation commands).

    1. The Structure

    A netlist is a simple text file.
    • First Line: Must be a title (e.g., "* My Circuit"). It's ignored by the simulator but helps you identify the file.
    • Body: List your components line by line.
    • Last Line: Must be .end to mark the file's completion.

    * My Circuit Title R1 1 0 1k V1 1 0 5 .end

    2. Supported Components

    Imagine your circuit is a Lego set. Here are the blocks you can use:
    Basic Syntax: Name Node1 Node2 Value

    • Resistor (R): R1 1 2 1k (1k Ohm)
    • Capacitor (C): C1 2 0 1u (1u Farad)
    • Inductor (L): L1 2 3 10m (10m Henry)
    • Voltage (V): V1 1 0 dc 5 (5V DC Source)
    • Diode (D): D1 1 2 model_name
    • MOSFET (M): M1 D G S B model w=10u l=1u

    *Node 0 is always Ground (GND).

    * My Circuit Parts R1 1 2 10k C1 2 0 1u L1 2 3 1m * DC Source (5V) V1 1 0 dc 5 * Pulse (Clock Signal) V2 3 0 pulse 0 5 1n 1n 1n 10u 20u .end

    3. Transient Analysis (Time)

    Want to see how voltage changes over time? Use Transient Analysis!
    • .tran [step] [stop]
    • Example: .tran 10u 5m scans for 5ms with 10us steps.
    • Use PULSE source to create switching signals (like a square wave).

    V1 1 0 PULSE(0 5 1m 1n 1n 1m 2m) .tran 10u 5m

    4. AC & DC Sweep

    AC Analysis: Checks frequency response (filters).
    .ac dec 10 1 100k (1Hz to 100kHz).

    DC Sweep: Checks response to specific voltages.
    .dc V1 0 5 0.1 (Sweep V1 from 0V to 5V).

    V1 1 0 AC 1 .ac dec 10 1 100k V2 1 0 DC 0 .dc V2 0 5 0.1
