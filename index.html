    Simulate circuits with simplicity.
    Mspice is a Python-based SPICE engine built for students and researchers — lightweight, portable, and fully open-source.

    Use .sp or .txt to create your circuit!

    Like Hspice, like Ngspice.

    About Us

    We are a team of four graduated students from the Department of Electrical Engineering at National Central University (NCU), Taiwan. This project, "Mspice," serves as our graduation capstone project, developed under the guidance of Prof. Chiu-Chih Chou.

    Why Mspice? While professional tools like Hspice and Ngspice are powerful, they can be intimidating for newcomers. Leveraging Python's intuitive syntax , we built this SPICE engine from scratch to gain a deeper understanding of circuit analysis algorithms. Our goal is to create a lightweight, beginner-friendly, and open-source tool that makes circuit simulation accessible to everyone—without the steep learning curve.

    Get-started

    Mspice uses SPICE netlists—text files describing circuits. Think of it as a recipe: you list components, connections, and cooking instructions (simulation commands).

    1. Netlist Rules (The Structure)

    A netlist is a simple text file.
    • First Line: Must be a title (e.g., "* My Circuit"). It's ignored by the simulator but helps you identify the file.
    • Body: one component per line.
    • Command: choose exactly one of .ac , .dc , .tran.
    • Last Line: Must be .end to mark the file's completion.

    * My Circuit Title R1 1 0 1k V1 1 0 5 .dc V1 0 5 0.1 .end

    2. Components & Sources (Supported Syntax)

    Imagine your circuit is a Lego set. Here are the blocks you can use:
    Basic Syntax: Name Node1 Node2 Value

    • • Resistor (R): R<name> <n1> <n2> <value> , e.g. R1 node1 node2 1k (1k Ohm)
    • • Capacitor (C): C<name> <n1> <n2> <value> , e.g. C1 node1 node2 1u (1u Farad)
    • • Inductor (L): L<name> <n1> <n2> <value> , e.g. L1 node1 node2 10m (10m Henry)
    • • Voltage (V): V<name> <n1> <n2> <value> , e.g. V1 node1 node2 dc 5 (5V DC Source)
    • • Diode (D): D<name> <n1> <n2> <value> , e.g. D1 node1 node2 myDiode
    • • MOSFET (M): M<name> <D> <G> <S> <B> <modelname> w=<width> l=<length> , e.g. M1 node1 node2 node3 node4 myMOS w=10u l=1u

    *Node 0 is always Ground (GND).

    Voltage sources:

    • • DC: V<name> <n+> <n-> dc <value>
    • • Pulse: V<name> <n+> <n-> pulse <VL> <VH> <delay> <rise> <fall> <width> <period>
    • • Sin: V<name> <n+> <n-> sin <offset> <amp> <freq> <delay>
    * My Circuit Parts
    R1 1 2 10k
    C1 2 0 1u
    L1 2 3 1m
    * DC Source (5V)
    V1 1 0 dc 5
    * Pulse (Clock Signal)
    V2 3 0 pulse 0 5 1n 1n 1n 10u 20u
    .end

    4. Choose Your Simulation!

    Mspice supports AC, DC sweep, and Transient. Pick one command and place it before .end.

    AC sweep:

    .ac <variation> <points> <f_start> <f_stop>
    e.g. .ac dec 10 1 100k

    DC sweep:

    .dc <source> <start> <stop> <step>
    e.g. .dc V1 0 5 0.1

    Transient:

    .tran <step> <stop>
    e.g. .tran 10u 5m

    * AC example V1 in 0 sin 0 1 1k 0 .ac dec 10 1 100k .end

    5. Subcircuits (Advanced)

    To reuse a block, define a .subckt and call it with X.... External node names are mapped by position, and internal nodes are auto-renamed to avoid collisions (see the node-substitution rule in our report).

    * Define a subcircuit .subckt myRLC a b c R1 a b 1k L1 b int 1m C1 int c 1u .ends * Use the subcircuit XU1 n1 n2 0 myRLC .end
